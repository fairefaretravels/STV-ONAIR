Professor Griff

Date: Monday, June 15, 2026  ·  Time: 12:00 PM (Noon)  ·  Outlet: STV Radiomeltdown / The Static

Background

Professor Griff (Richard Griffin) is a founding member of Public Enemy, where he served as "Minister of Information." Beyond his work with the group, he's spent decades as a lecturer, author, and media commentator, speaking on hip-hop history, the music industry's inner workings, independent media, and community organizing. He's a frequent guest on podcasts and panels covering culture, politics, and the business side of entertainment.

Hip-Hop History Independent Media Music Industry Community & Activism

Angle for The Static

Frame this as a conversation about the past, present, and future of independent media and grassroots culture-building — tying his decades of experience with Public Enemy and as an independent voice to what STV Radiomeltdown and G4E Movement Studio are building in Lithonia. Good fit for a featured/cover card in this issue.

Suggested Questions

  1. Looking back at how Public Enemy built its platform outside the traditional industry machine, what lessons still apply to independent media and radio today?
  2. What does "ownership" mean to you when it comes to media — and how should up-and-coming creators think about owning their platform versus renting space on someone else's?
  3. How do you see the role of community radio and local stations like STV Radiomeltdown in keeping culture rooted in the neighborhoods that create it?
  4. What advice would you give to artists and DJs trying to build an audience without losing their voice along the way?
  5. What's a project or idea you're currently focused on that you'd want our listeners to know about?
Keep a couple of open follow-ups in reserve — Griff tends to go deep on whichever topic lands, so be ready to let the conversation breathe rather than rushing through the list.
